# Evaluation Rubric

This rubric scores how well clinical cognition survives across a referral-consult artifact pair. Scores should be assigned cautiously and supported by artifact evidence.

## Scoring Scale

- 0: absent or unrecoverable.
- 1: minimal preservation with major ambiguity.
- 2: partial preservation with important gaps or drift.
- 3: substantial preservation with minor gaps or drift.
- 4: strong preservation with clear traceability.

## Dimensions

### Reasoning Preservation

Assesses whether the rationale, evidence selection, and clinical logic remain traceable.

- 0: no reasoning is visible.
- 1: conclusion is visible but rationale is mostly absent.
- 2: some rationale is visible, but key steps are missing.
- 3: rationale is mostly traceable with limited gaps.
- 4: reasoning pathway is explicit and coherent across artifacts.

### Uncertainty Preservation

Assesses whether uncertainty, alternatives, and caveats remain visible.

- 0: uncertainty is absent despite apparent ambiguity.
- 1: uncertainty is implied but not stated.
- 2: some uncertainty is preserved, but alternatives are compressed.
- 3: uncertainty and major alternatives are mostly preserved.
- 4: uncertainty is explicit, specific, and traceable across artifacts.

### Treatment Intent Preservation

Assesses whether the purpose of action remains clear across referral and consultation.

- 0: treatment intent is absent or contradictory.
- 1: intent is vague or only implied.
- 2: intent is partially preserved but action logic is incomplete.
- 3: intent is clear with minor ambiguity.
- 4: treatment intent is explicit and consistently linked to reasoning.

### Cognitive Drift

Assesses the degree of meaningful change in diagnostic frame, mechanism, recommendation, or responsibility.

- 0: drift is severe and unexplained.
- 1: major drift with weak explanation.
- 2: moderate drift with partial explanation.
- 3: minor or well-explained drift.
- 4: no major drift, or drift is fully explained and justified by new evidence.

### Introduced Reasoning

Assesses whether downstream new cognition is identifiable and justified.

- 0: major new reasoning appears without source or rationale.
- 1: new reasoning appears with weak support.
- 2: new reasoning is partially supported but incompletely linked.
- 3: new reasoning is mostly supported and distinguishable from referral cognition.
- 4: new reasoning is explicit, well-supported, and clearly separated from inherited cognition.

## Interpretation Guidance

- Average scores can summarize a pair, but dimension-level scores are more informative.
- Low scores do not necessarily indicate poor clinical care; they may indicate limited documentation or artifact incompleteness.
- High cognitive drift can be appropriate when the specialist has new information or a legitimate reframing.
- Coders should document why each score was assigned.
- Disagreement between coders should be treated as a finding, especially when artifacts are ambiguous.
