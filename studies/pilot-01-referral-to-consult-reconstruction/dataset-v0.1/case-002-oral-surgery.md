### Final Assessment

Alveolar osteitis, localized socket infection, retained root fragment, referred temporomandibular pain.

### Treatment Recommendation

Irrigation, medicated dressing, analgesic plan, and review if symptoms fail to improve.

### Uncertainty Statements

Infection is unlikely today because there is no swelling, fever, purulence, or lymphadenopathy.

### Rationale

Exposed socket bone and classic timing support alveolar osteitis. Lack of systemic or local inflammatory signs argues against infection.

### Follow-up Plan

Return in 48 hours if pain remains severe or earlier if swelling, fever, or drainage develops.

## Ground Truth Reasoning Notes

The referring clinician was weighing two post-operative complications and referred because management diverged: dry socket requires local palliative care, while infection might need antibiotics or drainage. The uncertainty was driven by pain severity rather than objective infection signs.
