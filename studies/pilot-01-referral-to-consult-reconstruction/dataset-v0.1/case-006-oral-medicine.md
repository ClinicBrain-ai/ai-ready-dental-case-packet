# Case 006: Burning Mouth Symptoms

Synthetic educational case. No real patient information.

## Case ID

P1-V01-006

## Specialty Domain

Primary care dentistry -> Oral medicine.

## Referral Letter

### Referral Reason

Daily burning sensation of tongue and palate for two months.

### Suspected Diagnosis

Candidiasis versus burning mouth syndrome.

### Uncertainty Statements

Oral mucosa appears normal. Symptoms are persistent despite empiric antifungal treatment.

### Requested Consultation

Please evaluate for oral medicine causes and advise management.

### Contextual Details

Patient reports dry mouth sensation and taste disturbance. No visible plaques, ulcers, or erythema. Recent dental exam otherwise unremarkable.

### Timeline

Two months of burning symptoms. One week of antifungal rinse without improvement. Referral sent after normal visual exam.

## Specialist Consultation Note

### Final Assessment

Probable primary burning mouth syndrome after exclusion of visible mucosal disease.

### Differential Diagnosis

Burning mouth syndrome, xerostomia-related discomfort, nutritional deficiency, medication effect, candidiasis, neuropathic pain.

### Treatment Recommendation

Screen for nutritional deficiency and xerostomia contributors, review medications, provide reassurance, consider neuropathic pain management if screening is unrevealing.

### Uncertainty Statements

Diagnosis remains provisional until systemic contributors are excluded.

### Rationale

Normal mucosal exam and lack of response to antifungal therapy make candidiasis less likely. Persistent burning with taste disturbance suggests neuropathic or idiopathic process.

### Follow-up Plan

Order screening labs through primary clinician, saliva management advice, follow up after results.

## Ground Truth Reasoning Notes

The referring clinician initially treated for candidiasis because it was common and actionable, but normal mucosa and lack of response made that explanation weak. Referral was motivated by diagnostic uncertainty and need to distinguish mucosal disease from neuropathic or systemic causes.
