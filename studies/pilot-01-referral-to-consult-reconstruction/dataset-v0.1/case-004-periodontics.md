## Specialist Consultation Note

### Final Assessment

Stage III localized periodontitis with secondary occlusal trauma contributing to mobility.

### Differential Diagnosis

Localized periodontitis, occlusal trauma, endodontic-periodontal lesion, systemic modifier.

### Treatment Recommendation

Periodontal debridement, occlusal analysis, splinting consideration, risk-factor review, and re-evaluation after initial therapy.

### Uncertainty Statements

The exact contribution of occlusal trauma cannot be quantified until inflammation is reduced and mobility reassessed.

### Rationale

Vertical bone loss and pocketing indicate periodontitis. Mobility appears amplified by traumatic contacts, but inflammation is the primary disease process.

### Follow-up Plan

Initial periodontal therapy followed by 6-week re-evaluation and possible regenerative consultation if defects remain.

The referring clinician noticed mismatch between oral hygiene and tissue breakdown. The uncertainty centered on whether this was mainly inflammatory periodontal disease, mechanically amplified mobility, or another lesion type. Referral was made for staging, prognosis, and treatment sequencing.
