# Case 010: Iron Deficiency Anemia

Synthetic educational case. No real patient information.

## Case ID

P1-V01-010

## Specialty Domain

Primary care -> Gastroenterology.

## Referral Letter

### Referral Reason

New iron deficiency anemia with fatigue.

### Suspected Diagnosis

Occult gastrointestinal blood loss versus dietary deficiency.

### Uncertainty Statements

Dietary intake may be low, but anemia is new and stool testing is pending. I am uncertain whether endoscopic evaluation is needed now.

### Requested Consultation

Please evaluate need for upper and lower endoscopy.

### Contextual Details

Adult patient reports fatigue. Labs show low hemoglobin, low ferritin, and microcytosis. No overt bleeding. No abdominal pain.

### Timeline

Fatigue for two months. Labs obtained this week. Referral made after iron deficiency confirmed.

## Specialist Consultation Note

### Final Assessment

Iron deficiency anemia requiring gastrointestinal evaluation for occult blood loss.

### Differential Diagnosis

Colonic neoplasm, upper gastrointestinal bleeding source, malabsorption, dietary deficiency, medication-associated bleeding.

### Treatment Recommendation

Bidirectional endoscopy, iron replacement, medication review, and follow-up after procedure results.

### Uncertainty Statements

Dietary deficiency may contribute, but it should not be assumed without excluding gastrointestinal sources.

### Rationale

New iron deficiency anemia in an adult warrants evaluation for occult gastrointestinal pathology even without overt bleeding.

### Follow-up Plan

Schedule endoscopy and colonoscopy, recheck blood counts, review pathology and findings.

## Ground Truth Reasoning Notes

The referring clinician considered benign dietary explanation but recognized that new iron deficiency anemia can indicate occult GI pathology. Referral was motivated by uncertainty about threshold for invasive evaluation and desire for specialist-guided workup.
