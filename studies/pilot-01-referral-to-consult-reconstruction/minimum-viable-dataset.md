# Minimum Viable Dataset

This pilot can begin without proprietary datasets or immediate IRB-dependent institutional records. The first proof-of-concept should use public, simulated, or educational cases to test whether the schema, protocol, coding framework, and rubric are workable.

## Possible Sources

### Published Case Reports

Published case reports may contain referral-like narratives and specialist assessment sections. They are useful for early testing but may be retrospectively polished and may not include true referral letters.

### Educational Cases

Medical, dental, nursing, or interprofessional education cases often include staged presentation and consultation-style discussion. They can support controlled reconstruction exercises.

### Simulated Referral-Consult Pairs

Synthetic cases can be created specifically to include referral artifacts and consultation artifacts. They are useful for protocol testing because the intended reasoning path can be known to the study team.

### De-identified Institutional Records

Future studies may use real referral letters and specialist consultation notes after appropriate institutional approvals, privacy review, and data governance.

## Target Sample Sizes

### 10 Pairs

Purpose: proof of concept.

Expected use: test whether coders can apply the schema, protocol, and rubric consistently.

Limitation: too small for stable pattern claims.

### 25 Pairs

Purpose: feasibility pilot.

Expected use: identify common transformation categories and refine coding rules.

Limitation: still likely specialty-specific and vulnerable to case selection bias.

### 50 Pairs

Purpose: exploratory analysis.

Expected use: compare transformation patterns across case types or artifact quality levels.

Limitation: may support descriptive findings but not broad generalization.

### 100 Pairs

Purpose: stronger empirical pilot.

Expected use: estimate frequency of preservation, uncertainty loss, reframing, and new cognition within a defined domain.

Limitation: still requires careful domain boundaries and may need institutional review if real records are used.

## Expected Limitations

- Public cases may not include authentic referral letters.
- Educational cases may be too clean compared with real workflows.
- Simulated cases may reflect the assumptions of their creators.
- De-identified records may omit context needed for reconstruction.
- Artifact quality may vary more than clinical cognition itself.
- The dataset can support partial reconstruction claims, not proof of original clinician thought.

## Recommended First Dataset

Start with 10 simulated or educational referral-consult pairs. Use them to test coding feasibility, clarify ambiguous categories, and train reviewers. After the protocol stabilizes, add 15 public or educational cases for a 25-pair feasibility pilot.
