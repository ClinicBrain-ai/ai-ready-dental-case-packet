# The Role of DCS in CCTL

DCS is no longer the central project.

The Dental Context Specification remains important because it was one of the original technical substrates that exposed the broader research question. It asked how dental claims, evidence, uncertainty, contradictions, provenance, and review state could remain traceable across agentic clinical workflows.

Within CCTL, DCS should now be understood as a candidate cognitive provenance representation layer.

DCS may help encode:

- clinical context;
- reasoning traces;
- cognitive transformations;
- uncertainty;
- supporting and contradicting evidence;
- workflow transitions;
- authorship and review status;
- downstream reinterpretation.

DCS remains valuable as a downstream representation experiment. It can be used to test whether structured claim provenance improves reconstruction, auditability, and accountability in human-AI workflows.

The primary research question is now broader than DCS. CCTL studies how clinical cognition transforms across distributed human-AI healthcare systems. DCS is one possible artifact for representing parts of that transformation, not the scientific endpoint.

Existing files that use DCS terminology are preserved as historical and experimental material. Renaming schemas, paths, or specification artifacts may be considered later only if it does not obscure the project history or break downstream usage.
