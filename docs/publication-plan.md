# Publication Plan

## Primary Article

Clinical Cognition Transformation: Studying Human-AI Clinical Cognition in Distributed Healthcare Systems

## Alternative Titles

- What Becomes of Clinical Reasoning When Humans and AI Think Together?
- From Clinical Reasoning to Human-AI Clinical Cognition
- Clinical Cognition Is Becoming Distributed

## Publication Direction

The primary article should define Clinical Cognition Transformation as a research program for studying Human-AI Clinical Cognition in distributed healthcare systems.

The article should not be framed as a DCS standards proposal, dental AI tooling paper, or software infrastructure report. DCS can appear as an origin case and candidate cognitive provenance layer.

## Core Argument

Clinical cognition is increasingly produced by distributed human-AI systems. Studying model performance alone is insufficient because clinical meaning, uncertainty, responsibility, and expertise transform as cognition moves through clinicians, AI systems, documentation artifacts, workflows, organizations, patients, and caregivers.

## Article Components

- Definition of Human-AI Clinical Cognition.
- Argument for clinical cognition as distributed cognition.
- Taxonomy of cognitive transformations.
- Methods for Cognitive Reconstruction.
- Role of Cognitive Provenance.
- Longitudinal questions about future clinical expertise.
- Origins in dental packet, DCS, and Reasoning Decay work.
